📖 About This Project

Why ImageShield Exists

Built to fight the growing epidemic of non-consensual image sharing — one scan at a time.

🔴 The Problem

The proliferation of digital platforms has led to unprecedented levels of non-consensual image sharing, particularly affecting women and children. Studies indicate that 1 in 8 social media users have experienced image-based sexual abuse, with 90% of victims being women.

This form of cybercrime extends beyond privacy violations, causing severe psychological trauma, reputation damage, and, in extreme cases, leading to self-harm. The anonymity of digital spaces makes it harder for perpetrators to be identified and prosecuted.

🛡️ Our Solution

ImageShield is a free, privacy-first web tool that empowers victims to take action. It performs an automated internet-wide scan for your photo, detects morphed or edited versions using AI hashing, and automatically generates a legal cybercrime complaint — ready to send with one click.

No technical expertise required. No cost. No data stored. Just protection.

⚙️ How the Technology Works

Image Hashing: When you upload a photo, we generate three types of fingerprints — MD5 (exact match), SHA-256 (cryptographic proof), and pHash (perceptual hash that detects visually similar versions even if cropped or colour-changed).

Reverse Image Search: We simultaneously query Google Vision API, TinEye, and SerpAPI to find every place your image appears online. Results are filtered and ranked by confidence score.

Morph Detection: Using perceptual hashing with a Hamming distance algorithm, we detect images that are visually similar but not identical — catching edited, face-swapped, or morphed versions.

Automated Complaint: All scan evidence is compiled into a legal complaint format and sent via secure SMTP to the selected government authorities.
